Customer Service Policy – Call Center Operations
1. Greeting Standards

All customer service representatives must begin calls with a professional greeting.

Required greeting format:

Introduce yourself
Mention company name
Offer assistance

Example:

"Thank you for calling ABC Telecom. My name is Sarah. How may I assist you today?"

Failure to provide a greeting reduces the Greeting Quality Score.

2. Identity Verification Policy

Agents must verify customer identity before accessing or modifying accounts.

Accepted verification methods:

Last 4 digits of SSN
Registered phone number
Account PIN
Date of birth

Agents must not disclose account information until verification is completed.

3. Empathy and Tone Guidelines

Agents must maintain a professional and empathetic tone throughout the call.

Empathy examples:

"I understand how frustrating this must be."
"Let me help resolve this for you."

The agent must avoid:

Interrupting the customer
Defensive language
Blaming the customer

Empathy is evaluated in the QA scoring rubric.

4. Problem Resolution Policy

Agents must follow these steps to resolve customer issues:

Listen carefully to the customer problem.
Confirm understanding of the issue.
Provide troubleshooting steps or solutions.
Confirm resolution with the customer.

Example confirmation:

"Does this solution resolve the issue for you?"

If the issue cannot be resolved, the agent must escalate to Tier 2 support.

5. Escalation Guidelines

Escalation should occur when:

Issue cannot be resolved within 10 minutes
Customer requests supervisor
Billing disputes exceed $100
Technical issues require engineering support

Agents must clearly explain escalation steps to the customer.

Example:

"I will escalate this to our technical team who will contact you within 24 hours."

6. Compliance and Privacy Policy

Agents must protect customer privacy at all times.

Prohibited actions:

Sharing personal data without verification
Recording sensitive data in notes
Discussing customer accounts with unauthorized persons

All calls are recorded for quality assurance and training purposes.

7. Call Closing Standards

Agents must close calls professionally.

Required steps:

Confirm issue resolution
Ask if further assistance is needed
Thank the customer

Example closing:

"Is there anything else I can assist you with today?"

"Thank you for calling ABC Telecom. Have a great day."

8. QA Scoring Rubric

Quality scoring agents should evaluate calls using the following rubric:

Category	Description	                            Score Range
Greeting	Proper introduction and professionalism	        1–10
Empathy	        Understanding customer emotions	                1–10
Professionalism	Tone and communication	                        1–10
Resolution	Effectiveness of problem solving	        1–10
Compliance	Adherence to verification and privacy rules	1–10

Overall QA Score = Average of all categories.
